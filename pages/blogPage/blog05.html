Team Pavaan Takes Flight: Unveiling Our Innovative Vertical Axis Wind Turbine

In our ongoing series chronicling the Civil Innovation & Technology Cell's journey, we're thrilled to delve into Project Pavaan, our groundbreaking entry for the Smart India Hackathon 2023!


The Challenge: Harnessing Wind Energy on a Smaller Scale

The problem statement presented by SIH was clear: "Development of a Small Scale Wind Energy Device." While traditional wind turbines often conjure images of massive, horizontal structures, Team Pavaan dared to think differently.


Introducing Pavaan: The Power of Vertical Axis Wind Technology (VAWT)

Our solution? A compact and innovative VAWT, a stark contrast to the horizontal axis wind turbines (HAWTs) dominating the landscape. Let's explore what sets VAWTs apart:


  • Vertical Advantage:

    Unlike HAWTs where the rotor and shaft rotate parallel to the wind, VAWTs have a perpendicular axis of rotation. This translates to several key benefits:

    • Simplified Components:

      Crucial components like the generator and gearbox are positioned near the ground, making maintenance a breeze compared to HAWTs with components hundreds of meters high.

    • Streamlined Blade Design:

      The VAWT's aerodynamics allow for simpler blade geometry. HAWT blades need complex tapering and twisting due to varying wind speeds along their length.

    • Reduced Noise:

      VAWT blade tip speeds are lower than HAWTs, resulting in significantly quieter operation.

    • Omnidirectional Power:

      The most significant advantage â€“ VAWTs capture wind from any direction, eliminating the need for wind-tracking mechanisms like yaw drives used in HAWTs. This also makes them ideal for areas with turbulent or gusty winds.

Beyond the Basics: Optimizing Efficiency for Urban Applications

While VAWTs boast undeniable advantages, a key area for improvement is efficiency compared to HAWTs. Team Pavaan tackled this challenge with two innovative solutions:


  • Variable Pitch Blades:

    Imagine blades that can adjust their angles based on wind conditions! This ingenious feature, when coupled with an anemometer measuring wind speed and direction, allows the intelligent controller (potentially using Arduino) to optimize blade pitch for maximum power and efficiency, especially during low wind speeds.

  • Enhanced Starting with the Magnus Effect:

    This fascinating principle involves a spinning object creating a sideways force. Team Pavaan harnessed this effect to provide Pavaan with an initial boost, potentially aiding self-starting capabilities in low wind conditions.

A Perfect Fit for Urban Environments

While Pavaan's design excels in various settings, it holds particular promise for urban environments like those found in Gujarat. Here's why:


  • Cost-Effective Solution:

    For urban applications where space is at a premium, Pavaan's compact design and minimal components make it a budget-friendly option compared to large-scale HAWTs.

  • Easy Installation and Maintenance:

    With components readily accessible near the ground, Pavaan offers effortless installation and reduces maintenance complexities.

  • Reduced Noise Pollution:

    The quieter operation of VAWTs makes them ideal for urban environments where noise reduction is a priority

  • Wind Direction Independence:

    No need to worry about wind direction! Pavaan's omnidirectional nature ensures consistent power generation, regardless of wind direction.
